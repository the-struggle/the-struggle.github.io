Strategies... for just a second.

Aug 7, 2018

So yesterday I mentioned several things that are necessary for running a trading algorithm, the first being strategies. These strategies, though, are no different (in a broad sense) than a strategy a human trader would implement. That being said, a computer and carry out more complex trades much faster, but if you can’t conceptualize the strategy before hand then you can’t tell the computer how to do it!

There are several basic approaches one could take, including

  • Trend following
  • Pairs trading
  • News based strategies
  • Strategies based on models (e.g. delta neutral strategies)
  • Mean reversion strategies
  • Momentum strategies as well as others. One of the great things about an automated system is that once it is organized you can use it to test your strategies in a systematic way. Although this could also be done for a human traded system, the automated system naturally lends itself to backtesting.

Given the plethora of strategies out there and the need to have some system set up to explore their effectiveness, I’m going to set aside strategies as an area of study for now. Once I have my backtesting system set up I will return to different strategy approaches.
